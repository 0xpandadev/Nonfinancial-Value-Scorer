# Source Register

Use this file as the upstream source map. The skill should distill source material into scoring definitions rather than copying full documents into context.

## Source Tiers

- **Tier 1 Core**: official frameworks and standards that define the scoring vocabulary.
- **Tier 2 Policy / Market Implementation**: official Japanese market or regulator material that shapes expected disclosures.
- **Tier 3 Investor Practice / Dialogue**: reports, declarations, and forum materials that reveal investor use cases.
- **Tier 4 Examples / Benchmarks**: company examples, awards, surveys, and sector notes used for comparison, not primary definitions.

## International Integrated Reporting

Primary source:
`https://www.ifrs.org/content/dam/ifrs/integrated-reporting/framework-and-translations/integratedreporting-framework-061024.pdf`

Role: International source for integrated reporting. It defines integrated reporting as explaining how strategy, governance, performance, and prospects lead to value creation, preservation, or erosion over short, medium, and long term. It also identifies the six capitals and content elements.

Use for:
- Six capitals.
- Strategic focus and future orientation.
- Connectivity of information.
- Materiality.
- Governance.
- Business model.
- Risks and opportunities.
- Strategy and resource allocation.
- Performance and outcomes.
- Outlook.
- Basis of preparation.

## SSBJ / ISSB

SSBJ standards page:
`https://www.ssb-j.jp/jp/ssbj_standards.html`

IFRS / ISSB overview:
`https://www.ifrs.org/sustainability/knowledge-hub/introduction-to-issb-and-ifrs-sustainability-disclosure-standards/`

Role: Sustainability-related financial disclosure architecture. Use SSBJ/ISSB for governance, strategy, risk management, metrics and targets, materiality, value chain, and business-model effects.

Use for:
- Sustainability-related risks and opportunities.
- Governance, strategy, risk management, metrics and targets.
- Materiality to investors, lenders, and other creditors.
- Industry-specific information.
- Climate-related disclosures through IFRS S2 / SSBJ climate standard.

## Source Ingestion Protocol

When adding a new source:

1. Record source title, publisher, URL, publication date, captured date, and tier.
2. Identify whether it defines a scoring rule, clarifies terminology, provides examples, or gives market/investor context.
3. Add only distilled definitions, score implications, and tags to reference files.
4. Keep the original URL in source references.
5. Mark any non-official or consulting/media source as Tier 3 or Tier 4.
6. Never let examples overwrite primary-framework definitions.
