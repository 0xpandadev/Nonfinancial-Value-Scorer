# Global Frameworks

Use this file when scoring non-Japanese companies or when the report uses global sustainability terminology instead of Japanese integrated-reporting vocabulary.

## Key Principle

Do not treat every global framework as the same kind of authority.

- **Enterprise-value / investor-useful disclosure**: ISSB IFRS S1/S2, SASB, IFRS Integrated Reporting.
- **Impact / stakeholder disclosure**: GRI, OECD Guidelines, UN Guiding Principles.
- **EU mandatory sustainability reporting**: ESRS under CSRD.
- **Topic accounting / target credibility**: GHG Protocol, SBTi, TNFD, CDP.

When scoring peer companies, lock the framework set before scoring. For example, do not score one company with ISSB/SASB and another with GRI/ESRS only unless the dashboard clearly flags the report-boundary difference.

## Score Use Rules

### ISSB / SASB

Use ISSB/SASB when the user wants investor-useful, enterprise-value-oriented scoring.

Upgrade scores when:

- Sustainability risks/opportunities are tied to cash flow, access to finance, cost of capital, business model, strategy, or value chain.
- Metrics and targets are industry-specific and comparable.
- Governance, strategy, risk management, metrics and targets are connected.

Downgrade scores when:

- The report lists sustainability topics but does not explain financial or strategic relevance.
- Metrics are not sector-relevant or are not comparable.

### GRI

Use GRI when the report emphasizes stakeholder impacts, impact materiality, and broad sustainability accountability.

Upgrade scores when:

- The organization explains actual/potential impacts and management approach.
- Topic disclosures include scope, boundary, data quality, and year-on-year performance.
- Impact evidence is linked back to strategy or risk management.

Downgrade scores when:

- The report is impact-rich but does not connect impacts to business model, strategy, or enterprise value.

### ESRS

Use ESRS when scoring EU companies or CSRD-style sustainability statements.

Upgrade scores when:

- Double materiality is explained with process, thresholds, governance, and stakeholder input.
- IROs (impacts, risks, opportunities) connect to strategy, policies, actions, targets, and metrics.
- Cross-cutting and topical standards are clearly scoped.

Downgrade scores when:

- The report has a compliance table but weak causal explanation or weak metric quality.

### GHG Protocol / SBTi / CDP

Use these for climate and environmental scoring precision.

Upgrade scores when:

- Scope 1, 2, and material Scope 3 emissions are disclosed with boundary, base year, methodology, and assurance status.
- Targets have coverage, baseline, timeframe, validation status, and progress.
- CDP-style governance, risk, strategy, and performance evidence is present.

Downgrade scores when:

- Only carbon-neutral claims are presented without inventory, target pathway, or residual-emission explanation.
- Offsets substitute for operational or value-chain reduction without explanation.

### OECD / UNGP

Use these for human rights, responsible supply chain, remedy, and due diligence.

Upgrade scores when:

- Salient human-rights or RBC risks are identified by geography, value chain, and stakeholder group.
- Due diligence includes policy, risk identification, prevention/mitigation, tracking, communication, grievance, and remedy.
- Board or executive accountability is visible.

Downgrade scores when:

- The report has a supplier code of conduct but no risk assessment, monitoring result, grievance mechanism, or remediation evidence.

## Recommended Global Source Stack

For a global company peer comparison, default to:

1. IFRS Integrated Reporting for value-creation narrative.
2. ISSB IFRS S1/S2 for investor-useful sustainability disclosure architecture.
3. SASB for industry-specific topic and metric selection.
4. GRI for impact completeness.
5. GHG Protocol and SBTi for climate targets and emissions credibility.
6. TNFD for nature-heavy sectors.
7. OECD Guidelines and UNGP for supply chain and human-rights-heavy sectors.
8. ESRS when the company is EU-regulated or publishes CSRD-style statements.

## Dashboard Requirement

If any of these frameworks are used, the dashboard should expose:

- Framework name.
- Source URL.
- Role in scoring.
- Which tags it supports.
- Whether it was used as a primary definition, metric benchmark, or supporting context.
