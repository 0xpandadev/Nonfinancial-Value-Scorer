# Scoring Rubric

Use a 100-point weighted score. Each tag is scored from 0 to 10, then multiplied by its weight.

Rubric version: `nonfinancial-value-scorer-2026-05`.

Do not change weights or score definitions inside a peer-comparison run. If a definition changes, create a new rubric version and mark old/new scores as not directly comparable.

## Why 0-10

The 0-10 scale is granular enough for peer comparison while still explainable. Use 0 for no evidence. Use 1-10 for evidence quality.

## Global Score Scale

| Score | Meaning | Typical Evidence |
|---:|---|---|
| 0 | No relevant evidence | No section or only unrelated text |
| 1 | Name-only mention | "Human capital is important" with no specifics |
| 2 | Generic policy | Basic policy or slogan, no company-specific mechanism |
| 3 | Company-specific narrative | Describes company issue, but lacks metrics or time axis |
| 4 | Structured disclosure | Has sections, initiatives, and some ownership or process |
| 5 | KPI exists | Has at least one relevant KPI, target, or result |
| 6 | Strategy connection | Shows how the topic supports strategy or business model |
| 7 | Time-series / target / progress | Shows baseline, target, progress, or multi-year trend |
| 8 | Value-creation causal link | Explains how the topic affects competitiveness, risk, revenue, margin, trust, resilience, or future cash flow |
| 9 | Governance and accountability | Board/management oversight, incentives, review cycle, or accountable owner is shown |
| 10 | Decision-grade disclosure | Quantified, comparable, time-series, governed, includes tradeoffs/negative information, and is useful for peer comparison |

## 100-Point Weights

| Tag ID | Label | Weight | Reason For Weight |
|---|---|---:|---|
| VCS | Value Creation Story | 18 | Central organizing logic; all non-financial disclosures should connect to how value is created over time |
| MAT | Materiality / Key Issues | 12 | Determines what is company-specific and strategically important |
| BMO | Business Model | 10 | Shows how inputs become outputs/outcomes and where non-financial capital matters |
| SIX | Six Capitals / Non-Financial Capital | 12 | Captures human, intellectual, social, and natural capital substance |
| STR | Strategy / Resource Allocation | 12 | Tests whether the story becomes allocation choices, investment, and execution priorities |
| KPI | KPI / Outcomes | 14 | Makes the disclosure testable and comparable |
| GOV | Governance / Accountability | 10 | Tests whether the board and management actually supervise the agenda |
| RSK | Risks / Opportunities | 6 | Captures downside and upside from sustainability, human rights, climate, nature, supply chain, economic security |
| QLT | Disclosure Quality | 6 | Ensures the report can be read, compared, and audited by users |

Total: 100.

## Weighting Philosophy

The score intentionally gives more weight to value story, materiality, strategy, KPI, and non-financial capital than to generic ESG breadth. The goal is to judge whether the report helps users understand future value creation, not whether the report mentions many topics.

Governance has a lower standalone weight than VCS/KPI because governance is also embedded as an upgrade condition inside each score. A report can score well only if governance appears in the relevant tags.

Risk/opportunity has a modest standalone weight because risks should also appear inside materiality, strategy, and KPI. Use this tag for explicit treatment of uncertainty, resilience, transition, and upside/downside.

## Tag Rubrics

### VCS: Value Creation Story

Definition: The report explains how values/purpose, external environment, strengths, business model, strategy, non-financial capital, KPI, governance, and dialogue connect to short-, medium-, and long-term value creation.

Score:
- 0: No coherent value story.
- 1-3: Purpose or value creation diagram exists but is generic.
- 4-6: Company-specific strengths, business model, and strategy are connected.
- 7-8: Non-financial capitals and KPIs are connected to strategic outcomes.
- 9-10: Includes time axis, tradeoffs, progress, governance, investor feedback, and negative/unfinished issues.

### MAT: Materiality / Key Issues

Definition: The report identifies and justifies the issues most important to the company's value creation and stakeholders/investors.

Score:
- 0: No materiality disclosure.
- 1-3: List of issues without process or rationale.
- 4-6: Selection process, stakeholder input, and strategic relevance are described.
- 7-8: Materiality links to risks/opportunities, KPIs, and business strategy.
- 9-10: Includes review cycle, board involvement, changed assumptions, and financial/non-financial implications.

### BMO: Business Model

Definition: The report explains how the company uses inputs to create outputs and outcomes through its business model.

Score:
- 0: No business model.
- 1-3: Generic business overview.
- 4-6: Inputs, outputs, customers, value chain, and profit logic are visible.
- 7-8: Business model connects to six capitals, competitive advantage, and risks/opportunities.
- 9-10: Segment-level or business-line logic, outcome data, tradeoffs, and future model evolution are shown.

### SIX: Six Capitals / Non-Financial Capital

Definition: The report shows how human, intellectual, social/relationship, and natural capital are formed, used, strengthened, or eroded.

Score:
- 0: No capital discussion.
- 1-3: Mentions people, technology, customers, or environment in generic ESG form.
- 4-6: Capital categories and initiatives are described with some metrics.
- 7-8: Capitals are tied to business model, strategy, outcomes, and time-series indicators.
- 9-10: Capital formation and erosion are both discussed, with governed investment logic and peer-comparable metrics.

### STR: Strategy / Resource Allocation

Definition: The report explains where the company is going and how resources are allocated to get there.

Score:
- 0: No strategy/resource allocation evidence.
- 1-3: Strategy slogans or mid-term plan headline only.
- 4-6: Growth themes, investments, and priorities are stated.
- 7-8: Resource allocation is linked to materiality, business model, and expected outcomes.
- 9-10: Includes capital/human/R&D/DX/M&A/withdrawal priorities, tradeoffs, decision criteria, and progress review.

### KPI: KPI / Outcomes

Definition: The report gives measurable indicators for strategy and non-financial value creation.

Score:
- 0: No relevant KPI.
- 1-3: One-off figures without target or context.
- 4-6: Targets and results exist for important topics.
- 7-8: Time-series, baseline, target, progress, and explanation are visible.
- 9-10: KPI hierarchy connects non-financial drivers to business outcomes and includes missed targets or corrective action.

### GOV: Governance / Accountability

Definition: The report shows who oversees and manages value creation, sustainability risks/opportunities, capital allocation, and non-financial capital.

Score:
- 0: No governance link.
- 1-3: Board composition only.
- 4-6: Committees, responsibilities, and risk oversight are described.
- 7-8: Board agenda, skills, monitoring cycle, and executive ownership are shown.
- 9-10: Incentives, director involvement, investor dialogue feedback, and issue-specific oversight are disclosed.

### RSK: Risks / Opportunities

Definition: The report identifies risks and opportunities affecting future value creation, including climate, nature, human rights, supply chain, technology, regulation, and economic security.

Score:
- 0: No relevant risk/opportunity disclosure.
- 1-3: Generic risk list.
- 4-6: Company-specific risks/opportunities and management process.
- 7-8: Scenario, value-chain, materiality, and strategy links.
- 9-10: Quantified exposure, resilience, mitigation/adaptation, opportunity capture, and progress metrics.

### QLT: Disclosure Quality

Definition: The report is useful for readers because it is specific, balanced, comparable, and evidence-based.

Score:
- 0: Not usable.
- 1-3: Promotional or mostly boilerplate.
- 4-6: Organized and partly quantitative.
- 7-8: Comparable, time-series, clearly scoped, and includes evidence.
- 9-10: Balanced positive/negative disclosure, consistent definitions, page-level traceability, and peer-comparison readiness.

## Confidence Haircuts

Apply after scoring:

- Low extraction quality: subtract 1-2 points from affected tags or mark confidence low.
- No page references: keep score but reduce confidence.
- Non-company-specific boilerplate: cap at 3.
- No KPI for a KPI-heavy topic: cap at 5.
- No strategy connection: cap at 6 for most tags.
- No evidence of governance for a mature disclosure: cap at 8.
- Different fiscal years in peer comparison: do not change score, but flag comparability risk.

## Repeatability Requirements

- Score tag-by-tag across all companies, not company-by-company when doing peer comparison.
- Use the same cap rules from `calibration-protocol.md` for every company.
- Keep a written reason for every score above 8 or below 3.
- Preserve score caps, overrides, and calibration notes in the score object.
- Re-run anchor examples when changing source documents, weights, or tag definitions.
