RUBRIC_REFERENCE

Scoring Criteria and Item Definitions

1-10 Scoring Scale

Tag Definitions

VCSValue Story

How clearly the report connects purpose, external environment, strengths, business model, strategy, KPIs, governance, and dialogue to short-, medium-, and long-term value creation.

Weight: -
MATMateriality

Whether the company identifies value-relevant and stakeholder-relevant material issues, explains prioritization, and shows the basis for selecting them.

Weight: -
BMOBusiness Model

Whether inputs, business activities, outputs, outcomes, customers, and the value chain are connected into an investable business model explanation.

Weight: -
SIXNon-Financial Capital

Whether human, intellectual, social/relationship, natural, and other non-financial capitals are explained from formation, use, and impairment perspectives.

Weight: -
STRStrategy Allocation

Whether the report explains where resources are allocated and how strategy is executed based on material issues and the business model.

Weight: -
KPIKPI Outcomes

Whether indicators, targets, actual results, and time series make strategy and non-financial value creation verifiable.

Weight: -
GOVGovernance

Whether the board and management oversee and manage value creation, risks, and non-financial capitals.

Weight: -
RSKRisk Opportunity

Whether risks and opportunities such as climate, nature, human rights, supply chain, technology, and regulation are linked to future value.

Weight: -
QLTDisclosure Quality

Whether disclosure is concrete, comparable, evidence-based, and balanced between positive and negative information.

Weight: -

1-10 Scoring Scale

  1. 10
    Benchmark

    Comparable, quantified, time-series disclosure with clear strategy linkage, governance ownership, negative/positive balance, and external evidence or assurance.

  2. 9
    Advanced

    Strong disclosure with only minor gaps in traceability, balance, or external validation.

  3. 8
    Strong

    Clear narrative, relevant KPIs, and credible evidence; some tradeoffs, downside, or comparability detail remains limited.

  4. 7
    Good

    Most required elements are present, but linkage across strategy, capital, KPI, and governance is uneven.

  5. 6
    Adequate

    Topic is disclosed with some evidence, but the investor-useful logic, targets, or outcomes are not fully developed.

  6. 5
    Partial

    Basic disclosure exists, but it is mostly descriptive and weakly tied to value creation or future performance.

  7. 4
    Thin

    Fragmented statements or isolated examples exist; evidence, scope, or accountability is unclear.

  8. 3
    Weak

    The item is mentioned, but without enough detail to support an assessment.

  9. 2
    Minimal

    Only generic policy language or slogans are present.

  10. 1
    Absent

    No meaningful report evidence was found for the scoring item.
